Liver Health Predictor

Before entering the diagnosis, know about them
  • ALB: Albumin - A protein produced by the liver and is measured to assess liver function and nutritional status.
  • ALP: Alkaline Phosphatase - An enzyme found in various tissues, including the liver, bones, and intestines. Elevated levels can indicate liver or bone disorders.
  • ALT: Alanine Aminotransferase - An enzyme found primarily in the liver. Elevated levels can indicate liver damage or disease
  • AST: Aspartate Aminotransferase - An enzyme found in various tissues, including the liver, heart, and muscles. Elevated levels can indicate liver or heart damage.
  • BIL: Bilirubin - A pigment produced during the breakdown of red blood cells. Elevated levels can indicate liver or bile duct problems.
  • CHE: Cholinesterase - An enzyme that is involved in the breakdown of acetylcholine. It is often measured to assess liver function or to monitor exposure to certain chemicals or medications.
  • CHOL: Cholesterol - Fatty substance found in the blood. High levels of cholesterol can increase the risk of heart disease.
  • CREA: Creatinine - A waste product generated by muscle metabolism. Creatinine levels are used to assess kidney function.
  • GGT: Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase - An enzyme found in various tissues, including the liver. Elevated levels can indicate liver damage or disease.
  • PROT: Protein - Refers to the total protein level in the blood. It can be used to assess nutritional status and liver function.
